The Story of Achi

A strategic journey across millennia

🌍
Roots

From Ghana to the World

Achi is an ancient strategy game played for centuries by the Ashanti people of Ghana. In the Akan language, it means "to arrive" — just like getting your pieces to the target!

🏛️
3rd-4th Century AD

Ancient Traces

Fragments found near Hadrian's Wall in England show that alignment games like Achi were played even during the Roman period. This game is truly ancient!

📖
1920s

Rattray's Discovery

British anthropologist Captain Rattray documented the games, laws, and spiritual world of the Ashanti in detail. He showed the world that Achi is not just a game, but a cultural heritage.

🗺️
1952

On the World Map

Game historian H.J.R. Murray officially recorded Achi in his book "A History of Board-Games Other Than Chess", placing it in the "alignment games" family.

⚖️
Rules

How to Play?

3x3 grid, 4 pieces for each player. First place the pieces, then slide them. Align three pieces vertically, horizontally, or diagonally to win! The strategy deepens when only one space is left.

🤝
Cousins

Relatives Worldwide

Shisima in Kenya, Tant Fant in India, Akidada in Nigeria... Different cultures, the same passion for alignment!

🎓
Modern Times

Achi in Education

In schools across Ghana, Achi is used to teach mathematics and strategy. Math problems are added to the points on the board — you must solve the problem to place your piece!

📱
Digital Era

From Sand to Screen

Achi boards drawn in the sand of Ghana now live in the digital world. You are now a part of this tradition — play and join history!
