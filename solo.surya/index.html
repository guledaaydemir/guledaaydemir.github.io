The language of Vedic astrology

Solo.Surya speaks classical Jyotish. Here are the terms the app uses every day — and what they actually mean.

Foundation

Nakshatra — the lunar mansion

A Nakshatra is one of 27 equal divisions of the sidereal zodiac, each spanning 13°20′ of the sky. The Moon transits one Nakshatra roughly every 24 hours. Each is further divided into four Padas (quarters of 3°20′). Your Janma Nakshatra — the lunar mansion the Moon occupied at your birth — is considered the most personal point in your chart. It rules your emotional nature, the dasha lord you're born under, and the deity, symbol and planetary energy that shape your life's tone.

Timing

Vimshottari Dasha — the 120-year cycle

Vimshottari Dasha is the primary Vedic timing system. It assigns a 120-year cycle of planetary rulerships beginning from your Janma Nakshatra. The cycle nests three deep: Mahadasha (major periods of 6–20 years), Antardasha (sub-periods within), and Pratyantardasha (sub-sub-periods). Where transits show daily weather, dasha shows the season — when career rises, when love arrives, when challenges concentrate.

Almanac

Panchang — the five limbs

The Vedic daily almanac: Tithi (lunar day), Vara (weekday), Nakshatra, Yoga (Sun–Moon angle) and Karana (half-tithi). Together they reveal which moments of the day are auspicious.

Charts

Vargas — divisional charts

Sub-charts that zoom into specific life areas: D1 Rasi (overall life), D9 Navamsa (marriage & dharma), D10 Dashamsa (career), and 13 more.

Calibration

Lahiri ayanamsa

The offset between the tropical and sidereal zodiacs. Solo.Surya defaults to Lahiri (Chitrapaksha) — the official ayanamsa of Indian Vedic astrology — for accurate sidereal positions.

Auspicious time

Muhurta & Rahu Kalam

Muhurta identifies auspicious windows for new beginnings. Rahu Kalam, Yamaganda and Gulika mark periods to avoid. The app surfaces both daily.

Compatibility

Ashtakoota Guna Milan

The eight-fold compatibility scoring of two charts. Solo.Surya computes all eight kootas and the Bhakoot/Nadi doshas for honest, classical synastry.

Geography

Astrocartography — planetary geography

Each planet in your chart projects four lines around the Earth — Ascendant, Descendant, Midheaven and Nadir. Standing under a Jupiter line feels different from standing under a Saturn line. Solo.Surya draws every line for every planet, so you can see which places amplify love, ambition, growth, or stillness for your chart specifically.

Frequently asked

Common questions about Vedic astrology and how Solo.Surya works.

Privacy

Is Solo.Surya really offline?

Yes — completely. Every calculation runs locally on your iPhone using a bundled Swiss Ephemeris. There are no accounts to create, no cloud to sync to, and no servers receiving your data. The app works in airplane mode. Your birth chart, partner profiles and history exist only on your device.

Accuracy

How accurate are the calculations?

Solo.Surya uses the Swiss Ephemeris — the same NASA-grade planetary engine used by professional astrology software — and defaults to the Lahiri (Chitrapaksha) ayanamsa, the standard for sidereal Vedic astrology. Dashas, divisional charts and panchang values are computed directly from classical formulae.

Difference

Vedic vs Western?

Vedic astrology uses the sidereal zodiac (aligned with actual constellations) and emphasises Nakshatras and Dasha timing. Western astrology uses the tropical zodiac.

Birth time

Why is birth time critical?

Your Ascendant and Dasha balance can shift with even a 4-minute difference. Accurate birth time = accurate Kundli and timing predictions.

Pricing

Is it free?

The core app — Kundli, Dasha, Nakshatra, Panchang — is free. A few advanced views are unlockable. No subscriptions.

Compatibility

Does Solo.Surya support friend & partner charts?

Yes. You can save unlimited partner and friend profiles locally and run Ashtakoota synastry, planetary aspect comparison and friendship-mapping between any two charts. Partners are stored only on your device, never uploaded anywhere.
